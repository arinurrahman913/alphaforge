# Company Analysis Playbook

## Purpose

The Company Analysis Playbook defines the standardized process for understanding a business before any financial, valuation, or investment conclusion is made.

A company should never be evaluated solely through financial numbers.

Understanding the business itself comes first.

---

# Objective

Answer one fundamental question:

"What kind of business are we analyzing?"

---

# Required Inputs

- Annual Report
- Investor Presentation
- Company Website
- Earnings Call
- Regulatory Filings
- Industry Reports

---

# Key Questions

## Business

- What does the company do?
- How does it make money?
- Who are its customers?
- What products or services generate most revenue?

---

## Business Model

- B2B or B2C?
- Asset-light or asset-heavy?
- Subscription or transaction?
- Platform or traditional business?

---

## Revenue Drivers

- What drives revenue growth?
- Is revenue recurring?
- What variables affect demand?

---

## Competitive Advantage

- What makes competitors struggle to copy this business?
- Does the company have network effects?
- Brand strength?
- Switching costs?
- Cost advantages?
- Intellectual property?

---

## Management

- Who leads the company?
- Capital allocation quality
- Incentive alignment
- Insider ownership

---

## Growth Drivers

- Organic growth
- Acquisition strategy
- Geographic expansion
- New products
- Market expansion

---

## Risks

- Customer concentration
- Supplier concentration
- Regulation
- Technology disruption
- Competition

---

# Expected Output

A complete business understanding including:

- Business Summary
- Revenue Model
- Competitive Advantages
- Growth Drivers
- Key Risks
- Overall Business Quality

---

# Confidence Assessment

High

Business model is fully understood.

Medium

Some information remains uncertain.

Low

Business model cannot yet be explained clearly.

---

# Common Mistakes

- Starting from financial ratios.
- Ignoring business quality.
- Assuming growth without evidence.
- Ignoring management quality.
- Ignoring competitive positioning.

---

# Philosophy

Understand the business before evaluating the numbers.

A great business can justify premium valuation.

A weak business rarely becomes a great investment.
