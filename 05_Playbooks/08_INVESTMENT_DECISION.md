# Investment Decision Framework

## Purpose

The Investment Decision Framework defines the standardized process for transforming an investment thesis into a disciplined investment decision.

A decision should never rely on conviction alone.

Every decision must be supported by structured evidence, transparent reasoning, and clearly understood risks.

---

# Objective

Answer one fundamental question:

"Should capital be allocated to this investment?"

---

# Decision Process

Every investment decision follows the same sequence.

Evidence

↓

Reasoning

↓

Risk Assessment

↓

Valuation

↓

Decision

↓

Confidence

---

# Decision Questions

## Business

Do we understand the business?

---

## Industry

Is the industry attractive?

---

## Competitive Position

Does the company possess durable competitive advantages?

---

## Financial Quality

Does financial performance support the thesis?

---

## Risk

Can the identified risks be accepted?

---

## Valuation

Does the current valuation provide sufficient margin of safety?

---

## Overall Decision

Should capital be allocated?

Possible outcomes:

- Invest
- Watchlist
- Reject

---

# Confidence Assessment

Confidence should reflect the quality of evidence rather than certainty.

High

Multiple independent sources support the decision.

Medium

Some assumptions remain uncertain.

Low

Evidence is insufficient.

---

# Definition of Done

A decision is complete only if:

✓ Every previous playbook is completed.

✓ Risks are acknowledged.

✓ Valuation supports the thesis.

✓ Key assumptions are documented.

✓ Confidence level is justified.

---

# Philosophy

Good decisions are the result of disciplined reasoning.

The quality of a decision should always be more important than the outcome of a single investment.
