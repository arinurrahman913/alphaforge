# Competitive Analysis Playbook

## Purpose

The Competitive Analysis Playbook defines the standardized process for evaluating a company's competitive position within its industry.

A strong company is not only financially successful.

It must also possess sustainable competitive advantages that are difficult for competitors to replicate.

---

# Objective

Answer one fundamental question:

"Why should this company continue to win against competitors over the long term?"

---

# Required Inputs

- Annual Reports
- Industry Reports
- Competitor Financial Statements
- Earnings Calls
- Market Share Reports
- Customer Research

---

# Analysis Framework

## Market Position

Questions:

- Is the company a market leader?
- What is its market share?
- Is market share growing?

---

## Competitive Advantage (Moat)

Evaluate:

- Brand Strength
- Network Effects
- Switching Costs
- Cost Advantage
- Intellectual Property
- Scale Advantage
- Regulatory Advantage

---

## Competitive Landscape

Identify:

- Direct Competitors
- Indirect Competitors
- Emerging Competitors
- Substitute Products

---

## Customer Position

Evaluate:

- Customer Loyalty
- Customer Concentration
- Pricing Power
- Customer Retention

---

## Innovation

Evaluate:

- Research & Development
- Product Innovation
- Technology Leadership
- Speed of Innovation

---

## Long-Term Sustainability

Questions:

- Can competitors replicate this business?
- What protects future profitability?
- What could weaken the company's position?

---

# Competitive Risks

Examples:

- Market Share Loss
- Technology Disruption
- Price Competition
- Customer Migration
- Regulatory Changes

---

# Expected Output

Produce:

- Competitive Position Summary
- Competitive Advantages
- Weaknesses
- Sustainability Assessment
- Overall Competitive Strength

---

# Confidence Assessment

High

Competitive advantages are supported by multiple sources and long-term evidence.

Medium

Some competitive factors remain uncertain.

Low

Competitive position cannot yet be confidently assessed.

---

# Definition of Done

Competitive Analysis is complete only if:

✓ Market position is identified.
✓ Competitive advantages are supported by evidence.
✓ Major competitors are analyzed.
✓ Long-term sustainability is evaluated.
✓ Competitive risks are documented.

---

# Philosophy

Financial performance is temporary.

Competitive advantage is durable.

Long-term investment success depends on sustainable competitive strength.
