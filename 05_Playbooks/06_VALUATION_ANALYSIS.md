# Valuation Analysis Playbook

## Purpose

The Valuation Analysis Playbook defines the standardized process for determining whether a company's current market price is justified by its intrinsic business value.

Valuation is not about finding cheap companies.

Valuation is about comparing price with value.

---

# Objective

Answer one fundamental question:

"Is the current market price attractive relative to the company's intrinsic value?"

---

# Required Inputs

- Financial Statements
- Historical Valuation
- Market Price
- Industry Multiples
- Growth Estimates
- Cash Flow Data

---

# Analysis Framework

## Market Price

Evaluate:

- Current Share Price
- Market Capitalization
- Enterprise Value

Questions:

- What is the market currently pricing?

---

## Relative Valuation

Evaluate:

- Price to Earnings (P/E)
- Price to Book (P/B)
- EV / EBITDA
- Price to Sales (P/S)

Questions:

- How does the company compare with peers?

---

## Intrinsic Valuation

Evaluate:

- Discounted Cash Flow (DCF)
- Owner Earnings
- Earnings Power
- Asset Value (when applicable)

Questions:

- What is the business actually worth?

---

## Growth Expectations

Evaluate:

- Revenue Growth
- EPS Growth
- Cash Flow Growth

Questions:

- Are current expectations realistic?

---

## Margin of Safety

Determine:

- Fair Value
- Current Discount or Premium
- Margin of Safety

---

# Valuation Risks

Examples:

- Overpriced Growth
- Excessive Optimism
- Unrealistic Assumptions
- Multiple Compression
- Interest Rate Sensitivity

---

# Expected Output

Produce:

- Valuation Summary
- Fair Value Estimate
- Margin of Safety
- Valuation Risks
- Overall Valuation Assessment

---

# Confidence Assessment

High

Valuation is supported by multiple independent methods.

Medium

Some assumptions remain uncertain.

Low

Valuation depends heavily on uncertain forecasts.

---

# Definition of Done

Valuation Analysis is complete only if:

✓ Multiple valuation methods are considered.
✓ Fair value is estimated.
✓ Margin of safety is calculated.
✓ Valuation assumptions are explained.
✓ Risks are identified.

---

# Philosophy

Price is what you pay.

Value is what you receive.

A wonderful business is not always a wonderful investment if purchased at the wrong price.
