# Risk Analysis Playbook

## Purpose

The Risk Analysis Playbook defines the standardized process for identifying, evaluating, and monitoring risks that could negatively impact an investment thesis.

Every investment contains uncertainty.

The objective is not to eliminate risk.

The objective is to understand risk before making a decision.

---

# Objective

Answer one fundamental question:

"What could invalidate this investment thesis?"

---

# Required Inputs

- Annual Reports
- Earnings Calls
- Industry Reports
- Regulatory Filings
- Financial Statements
- Macroeconomic Data

---

# Risk Categories

## Business Risk

Evaluate:

- Business Model Risk
- Customer Concentration
- Supplier Dependence
- Product Concentration

---

## Industry Risk

Evaluate:

- Competition
- Industry Disruption
- Technology Change
- Demand Cyclicality

---

## Financial Risk

Evaluate:

- Debt Level
- Liquidity
- Cash Flow Stability
- Refinancing Risk

---

## Management Risk

Evaluate:

- Capital Allocation
- Governance
- Insider Selling
- Succession Planning

---

## Regulatory Risk

Evaluate:

- Government Policy
- Legal Issues
- Compliance
- Taxation

---

## Macro Risk

Evaluate:

- Interest Rate
- Inflation
- Currency
- Recession
- Geopolitical Risk

---

# Risk Assessment

For every identified risk determine:

- Probability
- Impact
- Time Horizon
- Mitigation Factors

---

# Expected Output

Produce:

- Risk Summary
- Highest Risks
- Probability Assessment
- Mitigation Factors
- Overall Risk Profile

---

# Confidence Assessment

High

Major risks are identified and supported by evidence.

Medium

Some uncertainty remains.

Low

Risk profile cannot yet be confidently assessed.

---

# Definition of Done

Risk Analysis is complete only if:

✓ Major risks are identified.
✓ Probability is estimated.
✓ Potential impact is explained.
✓ Mitigation factors are documented.
✓ Risk profile is supported by evidence.

---

# Philosophy

Investment is the management of uncertainty.

Understanding downside is as important as understanding upside.

A strong investment thesis must survive rigorous risk analysis.
