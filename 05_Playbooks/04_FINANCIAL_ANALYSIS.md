# Financial Analysis Playbook

## Purpose

The Financial Analysis Playbook defines the standardized process for evaluating a company's financial quality.

Financial analysis is not about collecting numbers.

It is about understanding the economic performance of a business over time.

Every financial metric should answer one question:

"What does this number tell us about the business?"

---

# Objective

Determine whether the company demonstrates high financial quality, sustainable growth, and efficient capital allocation.

---

# Required Inputs

- Annual Reports
- Quarterly Reports
- Financial Statements
- Historical Financial Data
- Earnings Calls

---

# Analysis Framework

## Revenue

Questions:

- Is revenue growing?
- Is growth consistent?
- What drives revenue?

---

## Profitability

Evaluate:

- Gross Margin
- Operating Margin
- Net Margin

Questions:

- Are margins improving?
- Why?

---

## Cash Flow

Evaluate:

- Operating Cash Flow
- Free Cash Flow
- Capital Expenditure

Questions:

- Does the business generate real cash?
- Is growth supported by cash generation?

---

## Balance Sheet

Evaluate:

- Cash Position
- Debt
- Liquidity
- Equity

Questions:

- Is the balance sheet healthy?
- Can the company survive difficult periods?

---

## Capital Efficiency

Evaluate:

- ROE
- ROA
- ROIC

Questions:

- Does management allocate capital efficiently?

---

## Growth

Evaluate:

- Revenue CAGR
- EPS CAGR
- Free Cash Flow CAGR

Questions:

- Is growth sustainable?
- What drives future growth?

---

# Financial Red Flags

Examples:

- Declining Revenue
- Declining Margins
- Negative Free Cash Flow
- Excessive Debt
- Continuous Share Dilution
- Weak Interest Coverage

---

# Expected Output

Produce:

- Financial Summary
- Profitability Assessment
- Cash Flow Assessment
- Balance Sheet Assessment
- Growth Assessment
- Overall Financial Quality

---

# Confidence Assessment

High

Financial quality is consistently supported by multiple years of data.

Medium

Some financial indicators remain uncertain.

Low

Financial performance cannot yet be confidently evaluated.

---

# Definition of Done

Financial Analysis is complete only if:

✓ Revenue trend is understood.
✓ Profitability is evaluated.
✓ Cash flow quality is assessed.
✓ Balance sheet health is verified.
✓ Growth sustainability is analyzed.
✓ Financial quality is supported by evidence.

---

# Philosophy

Numbers are evidence.

Financial quality tells the story of business execution.

Never evaluate a company using a single ratio.
