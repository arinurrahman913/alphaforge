# Decision Engine

> Converting validated investment reasoning into disciplined, explainable, and actionable investment decisions.

---

## Purpose

The Decision Engine is responsible for producing AlphaForge's final investment decision.

It evaluates validated reasoning, weighs opportunities against risks, applies AlphaForge's investment philosophy, and produces a transparent decision that can be fully explained and audited.

The Decision Engine does not collect data.

It does not perform reasoning.

It makes disciplined investment decisions.

---

## Mission

Transform Validated Reasoning Packages into standardized Decision Packages that represent AlphaForge's official investment conclusion.

---

## Philosophy

A decision is the result of disciplined reasoning.

Every investment decision must be:

- Explainable
- Evidence-Based
- Consistent
- Repeatable
- Risk Aware

The Decision Engine never guesses.

Every decision must be supported by validated reasoning.

---

## Primary Objective

Produce transparent investment decisions that remain consistent regardless of who executes the analysis.

---

## Responsibilities

The Decision Engine is responsible for:

- Reviewing validated reasoning
- Balancing opportunity versus risk
- Determining investment conviction
- Selecting the appropriate investment action
- Defining supporting rationale
- Identifying remaining uncertainties
- Generating standardized Decision Packages

---

## Decision Package

Every Decision Package should contain:

### Decision

Example:

- Strong Buy
- Buy
- Watchlist
- Hold
- Reduce
- Sell
- Avoid

---

### Investment Thesis

A concise explanation describing why the decision was reached.

---

### Supporting Evidence

Key evidence supporting the decision.

---

### Key Risks

Primary risks that could invalidate the thesis.

---

### Confidence Level

Example

- Very High
- High
- Moderate
- Low

---

### Required Monitoring

Important variables that must continue to be monitored.

---

### Review Trigger

Conditions that require a new analysis.

Examples:

- Earnings Release
- Guidance Revision
- Interest Rate Change
- Competitive Disruption
- Regulatory Changes

## Success Criteria

The Engine succeeds when:

- Every decision is supported by validated reasoning.
- Risks are explicitly documented.
- Confidence level is justified.
- Decision is reproducible.
- Investment thesis is explainable.

---

## Failure Conditions

The Engine must reject processing if:

- Validation failed.
- Critical uncertainty remains unresolved.
- Investment thesis is incomplete.
- Risk assessment is missing.
- Decision cannot be explained.

---

## Design Principles

- Reasoning before Decision
- No Decision without Validation
- Transparency over Complexity
- Consistency over Emotion
- Discipline over Prediction
