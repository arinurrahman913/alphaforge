# Evidence Engine

> Evaluating the quality and credibility of investment evidence before reasoning begins.

---

## Purpose

The Evidence Engine is responsible for evaluating, validating, and organizing investment evidence before it enters the reasoning process.

Its purpose is to determine whether the available evidence is sufficient, credible, relevant, and trustworthy for investment analysis.

The Evidence Engine never performs reasoning.

It evaluates evidence.

---

## Mission

Transform contextual information into standardized Evidence Packages that contain only verified and relevant investment evidence.

---

## Philosophy

Not every piece of information deserves equal weight.

Investment decisions should be supported by evidence rather than opinions.

The quality of reasoning depends directly on the quality of evidence.

Evidence must always be:

- Relevant
- Credible
- Verifiable
- Explainable
- Traceable

---

## Primary Objective

Provide a standardized Evidence Package containing the strongest available evidence required for investment reasoning.

---

## Responsibilities

The Evidence Engine is responsible for:

- Collecting investment evidence
- Evaluating evidence quality
- Measuring source credibility
- Identifying conflicting evidence
- Ranking evidence importance
- Detecting missing evidence
- Generating standardized Evidence Packages

## Success Criteria

The Engine succeeds when:

- Evidence is relevant.
- Evidence is verifiable.
- Source credibility is identified.
- Conflicting evidence is documented.
- Evidence quality is measurable.
- Output is reproducible.

---

## Failure Conditions

The Engine must reject processing if:

- Context Package is incomplete.
- Critical evidence is missing.
- Evidence cannot be verified.
- Source credibility cannot be determined.
- Evidence quality is below the required threshold.

---

## Design Principles

- Evidence before Opinion
- Quality over Quantity
- Source Credibility First
- Transparent Evaluation
- Explainability First
- Traceability Always
