# AlphaForge Financial Database

## Purpose

The Financial Database stores structured financial information for every company.

Its purpose is to provide accurate, historical, and standardized financial data to support investment analysis, valuation, and decision making.

Financial data represents the measurable performance of a business over time.

---

# Core Identity

Every financial record contains:

- Company ID
- Fiscal Year
- Fiscal Quarter
- Reporting Date
- Currency

---

# Income Statement

Track:

- Revenue
- Cost of Revenue
- Gross Profit
- Operating Income
- EBITDA
- Net Income
- Earnings Per Share (EPS)

---

# Balance Sheet

Track:

- Cash
- Short-Term Investments
- Total Assets
- Total Liabilities
- Shareholders Equity
- Debt
- Inventory

---

# Cash Flow Statement

Track:

- Operating Cash Flow
- Investing Cash Flow
- Financing Cash Flow
- Free Cash Flow
- Capital Expenditure

---

# Financial Ratios

Track:

- ROE
- ROA
- ROIC
- Gross Margin
- Operating Margin
- Net Margin
- Debt to Equity
- Current Ratio
- Quick Ratio
- Interest Coverage

---

# Growth Metrics

Track:

- Revenue Growth
- EPS Growth
- Free Cash Flow Growth
- Book Value Growth

---

# Historical Records

Maintain:

- Quarterly History
- Annual History
- Trend Analysis

---

# Relationships

Financial Data belongs to:

- Company

Supports:

- Valuation
- Research
- Investment Thesis
- Decision Engine

---

# Metadata

Every record contains:

- Source
- Retrieved Date
- Last Updated
- Version
- Verification Status

---

# AlphaForge Principle

Financial statements tell the story of a business.

Investment decisions should be based on long-term financial quality rather than short-term price movements.
